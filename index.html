Grab CEO Anthony Tan Raised Over S$200,000 for a charity

November 26, 2019 Michael Robinson Uncategorized Leave a comment

Anthony Tan, co-founder of Asia’s transportation network company has broken record for having raised the highest amount by an individual. Tan who is our Generation T 2017 honouree and a change-maker made history not just because of his known ride-hailing app, but for raising a sum of S$202,010 to support the Children’s Cancer Foundation’s annual outreach campaign, Hair for Hope.

This is a record-breaking total amount raised by an individual since the inauguration of the campaign in 2003 and more than twice Anthony’s initial target of S$80,000. Masayoshi Son, founder of Japan’s SoftBank Group and Grab investor also donated S$90,000. Uber CEO Dara Khosrowshahi donated S$10,000

Grab Singapore has raised S$329,663.67 at present, with 107 drivers and workers involved in the Hair For Hope satellite event.

On his LinkedIn profile, Anthony posted a note sharing that the cause was “a deeply personal one”. He shared that his mother-in-law was a cancer patient, and his wife Chloe was deeply affected when she saw her mother lose her hair.

“I am a soon-to-be father of three, and I can’t imagine the grief that these children and their families go through when they receive the diagnosis and start cancer treatment,” he stated.

“Every child deserves to be a child,” he wrote on Anthony’s Hair for Hope page. He also thanked his friends, family and business partners for supporting him in this cause. The CEO shaved his head for the campaign during a company leadership retreat in Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam.
